01 About

I am an Associate Professor at Utrecht University School of Economics, where I have taught and researched for two decades — first as a lecturer, then assistant professor, and since 2025 in my current role.

My research asks a single, stubborn question: what really moves investors? The textbook answer — rational expectations, perfect information — collides with the world I actually study: analysts whose forecasts anchor on round numbers, retail traders who copy financial influencers, fund managers who quietly rename their products to ride the ESG wave, and tourists who lose a few percent of their holiday budget to dynamic currency conversion screens at airport ATMs.

That mix of finance, psychology, and field data has taken me from Stellenbosch to Shanghai. It has also produced work that occasionally crosses over: into the pages of Het Financieele Dagblad, onto Dutch radio, and into the courtrooms of policy debate around consumer financial protection.

I teach in the MSc Banking & Finance — which I directed from 2019 to 2025 — and I was U.S.E. Teacher of the Year in 2020. I currently co-supervise one PhD student.

02 Research

Four threads that keep coming back.

  1. I

    Sustainable Finance & ESG

    Do ESG ratings move stock prices? Do fund managers truly change strategy when they rename a fund “sustainable” — or just the label on the door? My recent work tackles greenwashing, climate-risk pricing, and the dominant role of the environmental pillar.

    • 2026 Environmental ratings and stock returns Finance Research Letters
    • 2025 Renaming with purpose Best Paper J. of Int'l Money & Finance
    • 2025 ESG rating changes and stock returns J. of Int'l Money & Finance
  2. II

    Analysts, Forecasts & Reference Points

    My PhD asked whether security analysts' opinions actually matter — and the answer, it turns out, is more nuanced than either the efficient-markets crowd or the analyst-bashers would have you believe. Target prices act as reference points; recommendations move some markets more than others; and chartists, surprisingly, sometimes are artists.

    • 2017 Target prices as reference point and takeover completion J. of Behavioral & Exp. Finance
    • 2016 Are chartists artists? Int'l Review of Financial Analysis
    • 2014 The relevance of security analyst opinions PhD thesis · Koopmans Institute
  3. III

    Retail Investors & Financial Decision-Making

    Where finance meets consumer behavior. I have studied dynamic currency conversion at foreign ATMs (a quiet tax on the less financially literate), the rise of finfluencers and whether following their advice costs you returns, bank switching behavior, and the savings-rate puzzle.

    • 2025 Influencers and consumer financial decision-making Int'l J. of Consumer Studies
    • 2023 Dynamic currency conversion harms the less financially literate J. of Public Policy & Marketing
    • 2020 Bank switching and interest rates J. of Financial Services Research
  4. IV

    Crypto, Disclosure & Market Microstructure

    Newer markets, older biases. I have asked whether Bitcoin "experts" can be trusted, whether technical trading rules actually profit in crypto markets, and what happens when confidential short positions are accidentally disclosed to the public.

    • 2024 Non-standard errors Journal of Finance
    • 2022 Can Bitcoin investors profit from crypto experts? Finance Research Letters
    • 2019 Accidental disclosure of confidential short positions Finance Research Letters

04 Teaching & Impact

Twenty years in front of Utrecht's finance students.

I joined Utrecht University in 2005 as a lecturer and have taught here ever since — through three career stages and one PhD. From 2019 through 2025 I served as Program Director of the MSc in Banking & Finance; in 2022 I earned the Senior Teaching Qualification (SKOw). I co-supervise PhD students and review for ten+ journals.

I have lectured in seven countries as a visiting scholar — Stellenbosch, Fudan, Nyenrode, Victoria, Anton de Kom, Erasmus, and others — and bring back to Utrecht a comparative view of what financial education needs to look like in 2026.
